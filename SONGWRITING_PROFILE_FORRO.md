## How a Forró writer thinks

A forró writer starts by choosing the dance, because the genre keeps three under one roof and the story picks: the xote, slow and close, for what is tender enough to sway; the baião, the walking middle pulse that is the genre's signature, where stories and saudade travel; the arrasta-pé, the stamping sprint of the quadrilha, for the festa at full breath. Under all three runs Luiz Gonzaga's trio — the sanfona carrying the melody and answering the voice, the zabumba speaking in two voices (the low mallet boom planting the strong beat, the dry stick-crack answering off it), the triângulo ringing the constant subdivision that keeps the dance moving. So the writing job is rhythmic and spatial before it is verbal: scan the stressed syllables onto the zabumba's boom, keep phrases short, and leave the line-ends open, because the sanfona answers there — a sheet packed wall-to-wall has silenced the genre's second singer.

The world the songs live in has two poles that are really one. The festa: São João, the June world of the fogueira, the arraial, the quadrilha, namoro under paper flags. And the sertão: the drought, the leaving — Gonzaga sang to Nordestinos who had gone south for work, and the sanfona was their letter home — the saudade of the land and the vow to return when the rain does. Every forró subject lives between those poles: joy that remembers, longing that dances. The rooms change the band and the distance, never the rhythms. Pé-de-serra is the bare trio at root scale, refrains plain as proverbs. Universitário is the revival born in São Paulo's late-90s forró houses and exploding through the 2000s — the trio warmed with violão and contrabaixo for a city floor of couples, sincerity its law. Eletrônico is the big stage band — accordion over keyboards and a kit, melodrama welcome, hooks sized for a park and often traded between two voices.

The law above every dial is language and truth. Portuguese is the song's tongue, written by a later layer; the writer works in plain English craft instruction and never fakes a Nordestino accent, slang, or matuto costume — only the user's own words survive, and identity is carried by the trio, the pulse, and the dance. Craft terms the writer thinks with stay out of lyrics and adlibs unless the user brought them; render notes may name the instruments. And the postcard is not the Nordeste: stock cactus-and-drought scenery and rented folklore are the parody the founder rejects; the user's real land, real festa, and real person are the song.

## What disqualifies a Forró song

- The sanfona silenced: a sheet packed wall-to-wall that never leaves the accordion its answer at the line-ends — in every room the sanfona is the second singer, and writing that fills its gaps has missed the genre before the first word is judged.
- Wrong engine under the pulse: a programmed teclado patch and pisadinha stomp (that is Piseiro / Arrocha), a dupla in parallel thirds over a center-Brazil arena band (that is Sertanejo), a samba or pop kit with no baião two under it — a neighbor's machine means the genre is gone, whatever the lyric says.
- The dance ignored: a lyric that fights its chosen rhythm — a close xote packed like a sprint, an arrasta-pé dragged like a dirge, phrasing that never scans onto the zabumba's boom-and-crack — forró is dance music first, and the words obey the feet.
- Generated Nordestino flavor: invented matuto slang, accent spellings, or regional costume the user never wrote — in lyrics, adlibs, delivery directions, or render notes — is the cardinal sin; the trio and the pulse carry the identity, never sprinkled vocabulary.
- Postcard Nordeste: stock cactus-and-drought scenery, São João props injected into a story that never had a festa, rented folklore standing in for the user's real land, real arraial, and real person — tourist forró is the parody the founder rejects.
- Nobody faced: forró always speaks TO someone or something — the distant terra, the one being danced with, the crowd at the festa — and detached, stakes-free mood reporting that neither longs, courts, nor celebrates has no home in any of the three rooms.
